My Core Pillars

These four pillars guide my vision for a stronger, more unified PA presence in California’s healthcare ecosystem.

Amplifying Grassroots & Student Voices

The future of our profession lies in our students. I will partner closely with PA student leaders to foster a robust presence at the State Capitol and AAPA, ensuring the next generation actively shapes the policies dictating their future practice.

Deepening Legislative Presence

Strengthening our profession requires a persistent, on-the-ground presence. Positioned locally near the Capitol, I will regularly attend healthcare hearings and cultivate relationships with legislative aides. We must be the trusted resources in the room before decisions are finalized.

Building Strategic Healthcare Alliances

Solidifying our role means building strong relationships with key allies. I will initiate targeted outreach with newly formed PA unions, RNs, MD/DOs, PTs, and others to cultivate a collaborative environment that champions the value PAs bring to patient care.

Leveraging Data and Innovation

Young, Innovative Leadership

Co-Leading PAs-CAN!

I believe that to fully integrate PAs into every aspect of California’s healthcare system, CAPA must focus its energy on strengthening and promoting the PA Profession. True integration requires us to be visible, vocal, and deeply connected at every table where healthcare decisions are made—whether at medical conferences, during bill considerations, or within interdisciplinary clinical teams. As Vice President, my vision for advancing this core area relies on a proactive strategy that mobilizes our students, builds strategic alliances, deepens our legislative presence, and amplifies our grassroots initiatives. First, the future of our profession lies in our students. We must leverage the tremendous energy and strength in numbers that our PA students bring across California. By fostering a much larger student presence at the State Capitol and at local events, we can ensure the next generation is actively shaping the policies that will dictate their future practice. I am committed to partnering closely with leaders like Deon Kidd to spearhead this effort, ensuring CAPA leadership and our students are highly visible, united advocates. Second, promoting our profession means solidifying our role within the broader healthcare team. I will develop a targeted outreach strategy to build strong relationships with key allies across every major healthcare profession. By initiating personal outreach and casual coffee chats with representatives from the newly formed PA unions, RNs, MDs, and PTs, we can cultivate a collaborative environment that recognizes and champions the essential value PAs bring to patient care. Third, strengthening our profession requires a persistent, on-the-ground presence where legislation is crafted. Positioned locally near the Capitol, I will regularly attend healthcare committee hearings. Becoming intimately familiar with emerging legislation and building personal, working relationships with the legislative aides who advise key senators is crucial. We cannot simply react to healthcare decisions; we must be the trusted resources in the room before those decisions are finalized. Finally, promotion is fueled by robust grassroots involvement. I plan to build a more synergistic relationship with AAPA, ensuring our state efforts align with and amplify national movements. By working directly with PA student leaders to organize and run campaigns like "PAs Can," we can create a powerful, unified voice that resonates from local clinics to the highest levels of state government. By empowering our students, uniting with allied health professions, engaging directly with legislative committees, and strengthening our grassroots network, CAPA can achieve its vision of total integration and secure a stronger future for all California PAs..
