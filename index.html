A Bold, Innovative Approach to Leadership

With extensive clinical experience in Emergency Medicine and as a PhD student researching health services and computational science, I bring a unique, forward-thinking perspective to the table. True integration requires us to be visible, vocal, and deeply connected at every table where healthcare decisions are made.

I am ready to be a vocal, forward-thinking advocate for all California PAs, bridging the gap between established leadership and the fresh perspectives our profession needs to grow.

My Core Pillars

These four pillars guide my vision for a stronger, more unified PA presence in California’s healthcare ecosystem.

Amplifying Grassroots & Student Voices

The future of our profession lies in our students. I will partner closely with PA student leaders to foster a robust presence at the State Capitol and AAPA, ensuring the next generation actively shapes the policies dictating their future practice.

Deepening Legislative Presence

Strengthening our profession requires a persistent, on-the-ground presence. Positioned locally near the Capitol, I will regularly attend healthcare hearings and cultivate relationships with legislative aides. We must be the trusted resources in the room before decisions are finalized.

Building Strategic Healthcare Alliances

Solidifying our role means building strong relationships with key allies. I will initiate targeted outreach with newly formed PA unions, RNs, MD/DOs, PTs, and others to cultivate a collaborative environment that champions the value PAs bring to patient care.

Leveraging Data and Innovation

My deep engagement with big data and healthcare technology equips me to leverage cutting-edge tools to expand CAPA’s scope, reach, and educational initiatives. Together, we can utilize geospatial analysis and health services data to break down legislative barriers.

Proven Leadership in Action

Co-Leading PAs-CAN!

Last year, I co-led the California Physician Associate Students: Collaborate in Advocacy Now (PAs-CAN!) grassroots initiative. Recognizing that California leads the nation with 633 designated healthcare shortage areas, we launched a multi-tiered campaign to mobilize constituent meetings between PAs and newly elected state legislators.

  • Coordinated 31 separate legislative meetings spanning 16 Assembly and 15 Senate districts.
  • Mobilized students from 13 different California PA programs alongside certified PAs.
  • Proved that organizing our collective voices is the most effective way to drive state-level advocacy.
